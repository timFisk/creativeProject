Top Roping

Learn to do this first.

This is probably the best way to get into the world of climbing. When you top rope you are being supported by the rope the entire time. There is no real risk of taking a big fall. This makes it an great place to learn the skills needed to move onto lead climbing.

To get started here all you really need are some friends to take you. Someone that has an extra harness and wants to teach you. Doesn't matter if it's in a gym or outside just climb and see if you like it. Outside is better though.

Lead Climbing

This is when the blood gets pumping.

In this style of climbing the climber is not supported by the rope. They have to clip into set anchors up the face. This makes it harder. Oh and you can fall 10-15ft.

At this point you will be asked by people if you can take them climbing. This means you'll probably want to start collecting some of your own gear. Harnesses, shoes, ropes, caribeners, belays, and quickdraws. Careful it's easy to spend a ton.
